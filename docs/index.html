Abstract

Deterministic deep learning models for time series forecasting produce single-point predictions that fail to communicate the inherent uncertainty of future observations. We present DecompProbFormer, an encoder-only Transformer that combines two complementary inductive biases: (A) a heteroscedastic Gaussian output head trained with negative log-likelihood, which directly models aleatoric uncertainty by predicting both mean μ and log-standard-deviation log σ per horizon step; and (B) a learnable moving-average seasonal decomposition module that separates trend from seasonal residuals before encoding, improving non-stationary signal modelling.

We conduct a systematic ablation across five model variants — LSTM baseline, deterministic Transformer, Trans+A, Trans+B, and the full Trans+A+B — on two benchmark datasets (ETTh1 and ETTh2). The full model achieves the best MSE on both datasets (ETTh1: 0.0451, ETTh2: 0.1118), reducing ETTh2 error by 14% relative to the LSTM baseline. Epistemic uncertainty is further quantified via MC Dropout at inference, enabling decomposed aleatoric and epistemic confidence intervals. Our results demonstrate that seasonal decomposition contributes the larger accuracy gain, while the Gaussian head adds calibrated uncertainty estimates at negligible accuracy cost.

Key Findings

01

Seasonal decomposition (Module B) is the primary driver of accuracy. Trans+B alone achieves the best deterministic MSE on ETTh1 (0.0449), outperforming the standard Transformer (0.0469) without any probabilistic component.

02

The Gaussian head (Module A) adds uncertainty at negligible accuracy cost. Trans+A matches the deterministic Transformer in MSE while providing calibrated σ estimates, consistent with NLL training balancing accuracy and spread.

03

The full model (Trans+A+B) achieves best MSE on both datasets and best NLL on ETTh1 (−0.1944), confirming that decomposition and probabilistic head are complementary.

04

ETTh2 is substantially harder. LSTM MSE 0.1303 vs ETTh1 0.0467. Trans+A+B reduces ETTh2 error by 14% over LSTM, the largest relative gain across all comparisons.

Architecture

  • Input projection: Linear(1 → d_model=64)
  • Positional encoding: Learnable nn.Embedding
  • Encoder: 2 × TransformerEncoderLayer (nhead=4, ff=256, dropout=0.2)
  • Module A: Two linear heads → (μ, log σ) per horizon step
  • Module B: Moving-avg decomposition (kernel=25); seasonal → encoder, trend → additive skip
  • Loss: Gaussian NLL (heteroscedastic)

Training Setup

  • Dataset split: 70 / 10 / 20 (train / val / test)
  • Normalisation: StandardScaler fit on train
  • Optimiser: Adam, lr=1e-3
  • Scheduler: ReduceLROnPlateau (patience=7, ×0.5)
  • Early stopping: patience=15 epochs on val NLL / MSE
  • MC Dropout: 50 stochastic passes at inference
